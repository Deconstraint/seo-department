# Digital PR Playbook

> **Tactic:** Digital PR (Data-Driven PR, Reactive PR, Creative Campaigns)
> **Difficulty:** High | **Scalability:** Medium | **Link Quality:** Very High
> **Time to First Link:** 2–12 weeks depending on campaign type

---

## 1. When to Use This Tactic

Use Digital PR when:
- The client needs high-authority links from national/international news publications (DR 70+)
- You want to earn links at scale from a single campaign (one campaign can earn 20–200+ links)
- The client has newsworthy data, research, or a compelling creative angle
- You need to build brand authority in addition to SEO
- Client operates in a niche with active media coverage (finance, health, tech, lifestyle, travel, etc.)

**Best Campaign Types by Situation:**
- **Data-led studies** — Best for clients who can survey audiences or own proprietary data
- **Index/rankings** — "Best cities for X" listicles journalists love to cover
- **Reactive/newsjacking** — Fast-turnaround comments/studies tied to breaking news
- **Tool/resource campaigns** — Interactive tools that become linkable assets
- **Expert commentary** — Quote placements with a named spokesperson

**Do NOT use Digital PR when:**
- The client has zero brand presence or credibility (build DR 30+ first via guest posting)
- You have no budget for content production or PR tools
- The client refuses to be named or won't allow a spokesperson
- The niche is too narrow for mainstream media interest (very B2B technical niches)

## 2. Prospecting Criteria & Qualification Framework

### Target Publication Types
| Tier | DR Range | Examples | Link Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | DR 80–90+ | Forbes, Business Insider, The Guardian, TechCrunch | Extremely high |
| Tier 2 | DR 60–80 | Industry trade publications, regional news, mid-size blogs | High |
| Tier 3 | DR 40–60 | Local news affiliates, niche outlets, newsletters with web presence | Medium-high |

### Journalist/Editor Qualification Criteria
- **Beat relevance:** Covers your client's industry or topic area (verify last 5–10 articles they've written)
- **Publication size:** Minimum 10,000 monthly site visitors
- **Active publishing:** Last byline within 30 days
- **Linkable:** Publication links out to external sources in articles (verify by checking recent articles)
- **Contact reachable:** Email available via Muck Rack, Prowly, Cision, or Twitter/LinkedIn DM

### Campaign Angle Qualification Checklist
Before investing in a campaign, validate the angle:
- [ ] Is there a clear data hook or news angle?
- [ ] Has this topic been covered by target publications in the last 12 months? (If yes, you need a fresh angle)
- [ ] Does the data tell a surprising or counterintuitive story?
- [ ] Is there a geographic angle that creates multiple coverage opportunities (city-by-city rankings)?
- [ ] Does the angle tie back naturally to the client's core topic/keyword cluster?
- [ ] Would a journalist read the headline and immediately want to write about it?
- [ ] Is the methodology transparent and defensible (journalists will ask)?

### Journalist Prospecting Methods

**Method 1: Muck Rack / Cision / Prowly**
- Search by beat (e.g., "personal finance," "small business," "health & wellness")
- Filter by outlet tier and recent activity
- Export contact list with email and Twitter handle

**Method 2: Twitter/X Monitoring**
- Follow relevant #journorequests and #prrequest hashtags
- Search: `"journalist" "looking for" [your topic]`
- Monitor HARO (see separate HARO playbook) for reactive opportunities

**Method 3: Competitor Coverage Analysis**
1. Search Google: `[Competitor Brand] site:forbes.com` (or other target outlets)
2. Find what campaigns earned them coverage
3. Reverse-engineer the angle and build a competing/updated version
4. Contact the same journalists who covered the competitor story

**Method 4: Google News Search**
- Search for articles covering your topic in the last 6 months
- Click through and find the journalist byline
- Build a list of journalists actively covering this beat

## 3. Step-by-Step Outreach Process

### Phase 1: Campaign Development (Weeks 1–2)

**Step 1: Ideate Campaign Angles**
- Brainstorm 10–15 angles using these frameworks:
  - **Superlatives:** Best/worst cities, states, companies, behaviors
  - **Trends:** How X has changed over 5/10/20 years
  - **Benchmarks:** How does [group] compare to [other group]?
  - **Myths vs Reality:** What people think vs what the data shows
  - **Cost of X:** How much does [behavior/problem] actually cost people?
  - **Seasonal angles:** Back-to-school spending, holiday shopping, summer travel
- Score each angle: Newsworthiness (1–5) + Relevance to client (1–5) + Feasibility (1–5)
- Select top 2–3 angles to develop

**Step 2: Collect or Commission Data**
- **Survey data:** Use Pollfish, Google Surveys, or Lucid for 500–2,000 respondents (~$500–2,000)
- **Proprietary data:** Work with client to extract anonymized data from their platform/CRM
- **Third-party data:** Scrape/aggregate publicly available government, census, or industry data
- **Freedom of Information (FOI):** Request public data from government sources (great for UK PR)
- Ensure data is clean, statistically significant, and methodology is documented

**Step 3: Build the Asset**
- Create a press release (300–500 words) with:
  - Attention-grabbing headline with the data hook
  - Lede paragraph with the most surprising finding
  - 3–5 key findings with supporting stats
  - Methodology section (sample size, dates, source)
  - Expert quote from client spokesperson
  - Link back to the full study/resource on client's site
- Create a dedicated landing page or blog post on client's site with full data
- Build visuals: infographic, data visualization, or interactive map (if budget allows)

**Step 4: Build Journalist Target List**
- Use Muck Rack or Cision to build a list of 50–200 journalists by beat
- Segment by outlet tier (Tier 1, 2, 3)
- Personalize outreach for Tier 1 targets (top 20 journalists)
- Use templated-but-personalized approach for Tier 2–3

### Phase 2: Outreach (Weeks 2–4)

**Step 5: Craft Personalized Pitches**
- Subject line: Lead with the most surprising data point
- Keep pitch under 150 words (journalists get hundreds of pitches/day)
- Lead with the news angle, not the client
- Include 2–3 key stats inline
- Link to the full study/press release
- Offer exclusive embargo or first look for Tier 1 targets

**Step 6: Embargo Strategy for Big Campaigns**
- Offer 1–3 Tier 1 journalists a 48-hour exclusive before general release
- This increases chances of major publication coverage before the story goes wide
- Once the embargo lifts, send to full list simultaneously

**Step 7: General Pitch Send**
- Send to Tier 1 personally, same day
- Send Tier 2–3 via PR outreach tool (Prowly, Cision, or Mailshake)
- Include full press release, link to assets, and spokesperson contact info
- Best send times: Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10 AM journalist's local time

**Step 8: Follow Up**
- Follow up once per journalist, 2–3 days after initial pitch
- Offer: a different angle, an exclusive comment, updated stats, or a spokesperson interview
- Do NOT follow up more than twice — it kills journalist relationships
- If a journalist picks up the story, send a thank-you note immediately

### Phase 3: Amplification & Link Harvesting (Weeks 4–8)

**Step 9: Monitor Coverage and Collect Links**
- Set up Google Alerts for campaign keywords and client brand
- Use Ahrefs Alerts for new backlinks to the asset URL
- Track all coverage in a media log (publication, journalist, URL, DR, do-follow status, date)
- Screenshot all coverage (publications sometimes delete articles)

**Step 10: Chase Unlinked Mentions**
- Some journalists will mention the data/client without linking
- Use Ahrefs Content Explorer or Brand24 to find unlinked mentions
- Email the journalist: "Hi [Name], thank you for covering our study! Would it be possible to add a link to the original research at [URL]? It would help your readers access the full data."
- Conversion rate on these requests: 20–40%

**Step 11: Republish and Re-pitch Seasonal Angles**
- Update the data annually and re-pitch as a "Year 2" study
- Re-pitch regional angles to local/regional outlets who missed the initial send
- Identify new journalist beats who could use the data for different story angles

## 4. Outreach Templates

### Template A: Data Campaign Cold Pitch

**Subject:** [Surprising Stat]: Americans [Shocking Finding], New Study Finds

Hi [First Name],

New research we've just released might be worth a quick look: [One-sentence surprising finding from your study].

Key findings:
- [Stat 1 — most surprising]
- [Stat 2 — second strongest hook]
- [Stat 3 — geographic or demographic angle]

The full study (with methodology and interactive data) is at: [URL]

We surveyed [X,XXX] [demographic] between [dates]. Happy to share the full dataset or connect you with [Expert Name at Client] for comment.

Is this something you'd cover?

[Your Name]
[Title] | [Agency/Client]
[Phone] | [Email]

---

### Template B: Expert Commentary / Reactive Pitch

**Subject:** Expert comment on [News Story You Just Saw]

Hi [First Name],

Saw your piece on [article topic] this morning — timely.

[Client Expert Name], [Title] at [Company], has a strong perspective on this: [1–2 sentence expert quote or angle].

Happy to arrange a quick call or send a longer written comment if useful for a follow-up.

[Your Name]
[Agency] on behalf of [Client]

---

### Template C: Exclusive Embargo Offer

**Subject:** Exclusive data: [Story hook] — embargo until [Date/Time]

Hi [First Name],

I have a data-driven story I think would be perfect for [Publication/Your beat] — and I wanted to offer you first look before we release it publicly on [Date].

**Story hook:** [One compelling sentence summary]

**Why it's timely:** [Tie to current news cycle or trend]

The full study includes [X,XXX] survey respondents and covers [geographic/demographic breakdown].

Can I send the full press release under embargo? Happy to arrange a spokesperson interview before the embargo lifts.

[Your Name]

---

### Template D: Unlinked Mention Follow-Up

**Subject:** Re: [Their Article Title] — quick request

Hi [First Name],

Thank you for mentioning our [research/study] in your recent piece — really appreciated the coverage.

One small ask: would it be possible to add a hyperlink to the original research at [URL]? It would help your readers access the full methodology and dataset.

Totally understand if not — just wanted to flag it.

Thanks again for the great article.

[Your Name]

## 5. Campaign Tracking & Management

### Media Log Columns
| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| Publication | Outlet name |
| Journalist | Writer's name |
| Article URL | Full URL of coverage |
| Publication Date | Date article went live |
| DR | Domain Rating of outlet |
| Do-Follow? | Yes / No |
| Link to Asset? | Does article link to our asset? |
| Anchor Text | What was used as anchor |
| Notes | Exclusive, embargo, syndicated |

### Campaign Dashboard Metrics (Track Weekly During Active Campaign)
- Pitches sent (cumulative)
- Response rate (%)
- Articles live (cumulative)
- Total links earned
- Do-follow link count
- Average DR of placements
- Traffic to asset page (via GA4)
- New referring domains (Ahrefs)

### Tools
- **PR Database:** Muck Rack, Cision, Prowly, ResponseSource (UK)
- **Email Outreach:** Prowly, Mailshake, or manual Gmail for personalized sends
- **Monitoring:** Google Alerts, Ahrefs Alerts, Brand24, Mention.com
- **Survey/Data:** Pollfish, Lucid, Google Surveys, Census Bureau data
- **Visualization:** Flourish, Datawrapper, Tableau Public
- **Tracking:** Ahrefs (backlinks), GA4 (traffic), Muck Rack (coverage)

## 6. Success Metrics & KPIs

### Primary KPIs
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Links per campaign | 20–200+ (depends on campaign budget/quality) |
| Average DR of placements | ≥ 60 |
| Tier 1 placements (DR 70+) | ≥ 5 per major campaign |
| % Do-Follow links | ≥ 70% (some news sites use nofollow) |
| Unlinked mention conversion rate | 20–40% |
| Asset page organic traffic growth | +500–5,000 visitors/month |
| Response rate to pitches | 5–15% (good for cold PR outreach) |

### Secondary KPIs
- Brand mention volume (Brand24/Mention.com — increase in brand awareness)
- Journalist relationship quality (repeat coverage from same journalists = success signal)
- Social shares of the campaign asset
- Domain authority change over 90 days
- Number of syndications (one article syndicated to 10 outlets = 10 links)

### Cost Per Link Benchmarks
- DIY data campaign: $500–2,000 in survey costs → 20–100 links = $10–100 per link
- Agency-produced campaign: $3,000–10,000 → 30–150 links = $30–80 per link
- Compare to paid links: $200–2,000 per paid guest post with risk

## 7. What to Avoid

### PR & SEO Mistakes
- **Pitching without a data hook** — Opinion-only pitches rarely earn coverage from major publications. Always anchor to data, a study, or a unique stat.
- **Over-pitching the same journalist** — More than 2 follow-ups on the same campaign burns the relationship permanently.
- **Using generic subject lines** — "New Study," "Press Release," or "Story Idea" kill open rates. Always lead with the most compelling stat.
- **Publishing the asset on a subpar page** — The asset page on the client's site must be polished, load fast, and be worthy of links. Journalists judge the source.
- **Ignoring unlinked mentions** — Up to 30% of PR coverage comes without a link. Always chase unlinked mentions; it's free link velocity.
- **Fabricating or inflating data** — If your methodology is called out publicly, it damages client reputation and the links will be removed.
- **Making the client the hero** — Journalists cover stories, not brands. Your press release should be 80% story, 20% client. Lead with the data.

### Legal & Ethical Issues
- **Plagiarizing existing studies** — Always cite third-party data sources and never repackage someone else's research as your own
- **Misrepresenting survey methodology** — Be transparent about sample size, demographics, and collection method
- **Over-claiming statistics** — "Americans spend $X billion on Y" requires solid sourcing
- **Using spokesperson quotes without approval** — Always get written approval for all quotes before pitching

# Prompt SEO — Playbook

> **The Frontier:** Prompt SEO is the newest and most forward-looking discipline in our AI SEO suite. As AI assistants become the primary interface for information discovery — embedded in apps, devices, operating systems, and workflows — the "query" is no longer a keyword; it's a natural language prompt. This playbook is how you win when the user is talking to an AI, not typing into a search box.

---

## What Is Prompt SEO and Why It Matters in 2026

Prompt SEO is the practice of engineering your brand, content, and digital presence to appear as the recommended answer, cited source, or preferred option when users interact with AI assistants via natural language prompts — whether in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Microsoft Copilot, Apple Intelligence, or any AI-embedded application.

Unlike traditional SEO (optimize for keyword queries) or GEO (optimize for AI-generated answer summaries), Prompt SEO specifically addresses the **conversational, contextual, and task-oriented nature of how humans interact with AI**:

- "Hey Gemini, recommend a good marketing agency for a B2B SaaS company"
- "Claude, help me find the best CRM for a 10-person team under $50/month"
- "Perplexity, what are the top 3 AI SEO agencies in 2026?"
- "Copilot, create a shortlist of vendors for our paid search program"

### The 2026 Landscape
- Apple Intelligence is now the default AI on all Apple devices — it processes millions of brand and product recommendation queries daily
- Microsoft Copilot is embedded in every Windows 11 installation and all Microsoft 365 apps
- AI assistants embedded in browsers (Chrome AI, Arc AI), productivity tools (Notion AI, Slack AI), and CRMs (Salesforce Einstein, HubSpot AI) are now key brand discovery surfaces
- The "prompt economy" is real: in 2026, an estimated 35% of all brand discovery happens via an AI assistant rather than a direct search
- The brands that appear in AI recommendations without being explicitly prompted by the user — because the AI "knows" they're the right fit — are winning the zero-click era

### What Makes Prompt SEO Unique
- **Contextual, not keyword-based:** AI prompts carry rich context (industry, company size, use case, budget) that traditional search doesn't
- **Recommendation-oriented:** Users ask AI to recommend, compare, and decide — not just inform
- **Continuous context:** In multi-turn conversations, your brand can be mentioned early and remain in context through the entire decision journey
- **Untraceable by traditional analytics:** Most AI assistant interactions don't generate referer data — you won't see them in GA4 without intentional attribution setup

## Step-by-Step Prompt SEO Process

### Step 1: Map the Prompt Landscape for Your Category
Before you can win in prompt results, you need to understand how people are prompting AI about your category.

**Actions:**
- Interview your last 20 customers: "Did you use an AI assistant in your research process? What did you ask?"
- Use ChatGPT and Claude to generate lists of common prompts in your category: "What are 20 prompts someone might ask when researching [your service/product category]?"
- Test these prompts in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity — document every response
- Build a "Prompt Universe" spreadsheet: prompt text | AI platform | does brand appear | what appears instead | sentiment
- Categorize prompts by intent: Discovery ("recommend X"), Comparison ("X vs Y"), Validation ("is X a good choice for Y"), Deep Research
- Identify "golden prompts" — high-intent prompts where you should appear but don't

**Output:** Prompt Landscape Map (a unique deliverable only Deconstraint provides)

---

### Step 2: Optimize Your Brand's "Prompt-Ready" Positioning
AI assistants need to understand what you do, who you're for, and why you're the right choice — in a way that maps to how people prompt.

**Actions:**
- Define your "Prompt Identity": a 2-3 sentence description of your brand written *as an AI would describe you to a user asking for a recommendation*
  - Example: "Deconstraint is an AI-native SEO agency specializing in helping B2B SaaS companies appear in AI-generated search results. They focus on GEO, LLM optimization, and AI SERP features — areas most traditional agencies don't cover."
- Publish this Prompt Identity on your website in a clearly structured format (homepage, About page, LinkedIn)
- Ensure your Prompt Identity answers the "who is this for" question explicitly — AI assistants use this to match your brand to user context
- Create "Use Case" pages that directly map your offering to specific prompts: "Best [your service] for [specific use case]"
- Add a "Who We Work With" section that explicitly names the company types, industries, sizes, and situations where you're the right choice

---

### Step 3: Build "Prompt-Triggering" Content Patterns
Certain content patterns are far more likely to be pulled into AI responses to recommendation prompts.

**High-Prompt-Trigger Content Types:**
1. **"Best [Category] for [Use Case]" Lists** — These directly match recommendation prompts
2. **Comparison Articles** — "X vs Y for [specific use case]" content is cited in comparison prompts
3. **"Is X Right for Me?" Guides** — Matches validation prompts
4. **Curated Tool/Vendor Lists** — AI uses these as reference when users ask for vendor recommendations
5. **Category Definition Content** — "What is [category]?" content establishes your authority in AI's knowledge base

**Actions:**
- Identify the top 10 recommendation prompts in your category
- Create a dedicated piece of content optimized for each prompt
- Structure content with clear "right for you if..." and "not right for you if..." sections (AI loves these for context-matching)
- Include specific use cases, company sizes, industries, and budget ranges — this enables AI to accurately recommend you in context

---

### Step 4: Position for "AI Recommendation Triggers"
AI assistants recommend brands based on specific trigger patterns they've learned from training data. Understand and optimize for them.

**Common AI Recommendation Triggers:**
- **Review aggregation:** AI recommends brands with strong review profiles on G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Google
- **Expert endorsement:** Brands mentioned positively by recognized experts in trusted publications
- **Category leadership signals:** Being listed in "top X" roundups by authoritative sources
- **Specificity of positioning:** Brands with a clear, specific positioning beat generalists in AI recommendations
- **Social proof concentration:** Volume and quality of testimonials, case studies, and client results

**Actions:**
- Build a systematic review generation program targeting G2, Capterra, and Google (minimum 25 reviews per platform)
- Pursue "top X" list placements in authoritative industry publications (these are heavily cited by AI recommendation systems)
- Develop 3-5 detailed case studies with specific results, client names (if permitted), and measurable outcomes
- Collect and publish expert endorsements from recognizable figures in your industry
- Create a comprehensive "Best [Your Category] Tools/Agencies" list that includes competitors — being the author of the authoritative list is a top-tier prompt SEO play

---

### Step 5: Optimize for Conversational Context Retention
In multi-turn AI conversations, early mentions of your brand can persist through the entire research session. Optimize for this.

**Actions:**
- Create "early-conversation" content that introduces your brand in the context of problem definition (before the user even knows what solution they need)
- Publish problem-awareness content: "Signs you need [your service]" — these appear early in exploratory AI conversations
- Ensure your brand is associated with the correct "problem language" — the specific words users use when first describing their problem to an AI
- Create content that bridges problem definition → solution category → your brand: a three-step semantic chain that AI can follow
- Build a "conversation starter" FAQ that anticipates the first questions a prospect would ask an AI about their problem

---

### Step 6: Leverage Prompt-Specific Platforms
Different AI platforms have different user prompt behaviors. Optimize for each.

**ChatGPT (140M+ daily users)**
- ChatGPT users often prompt with tasks: "Help me find a vendor for X" — create task-oriented content
- ChatGPT's Custom GPTs can be created for your brand — a GPT specialized in your category positions you as the authority
- ChatGPT plugins (evolving ecosystem) — build or integrate with relevant plugins if your product has an API
- ChatGPT memory feature: users can instruct ChatGPT to remember their preferences — content that teaches users to set brand preferences wins long-term

**Claude (Anthropic)**
- Claude users tend to be more technical and thoughtful — long-form, nuanced content performs better
- Claude prioritizes accuracy and will correct brand misinformation — ensure all published facts about your brand are accurate
- Claude with Projects feature can retain context across conversations — create brand documentation suitable for users to upload as project context
- Claude values expertise — thought leadership from named experts with verifiable credentials

**Gemini (Google)**
- Gemini integrates deeply with Google Workspace — for B2B, ensure your brand appears in contexts where people are doing work
- Gemini will reference Google Search results — traditional SEO and AI SEO compound here
- Gemini's Gems (custom AI modes) — create a branded Gem if relevant to your audience
- Google Maps and Business Profile feed into Gemini for local/business queries

**Perplexity**
- Perplexity users are research-oriented — deep, comprehensive, cited content wins
- Perplexity Spaces are branded research environments — create and maintain a Perplexity Space if possible
- Perplexity shows "sources" prominently — being cited = being visible to all users reading the answer
- Perplexity users often follow up with deeper questions — create content clusters that answer the full research arc

**Apple Intelligence**
- Integrates with Siri and all Apple apps — local business optimization is critical
- Apple Maps listing is a direct feed for local AI recommendations
- App Store presence with keyword-rich descriptions for app-based brands
- Apple's AI respects user privacy signals — content quality over quantity

**Microsoft Copilot (Enterprise)**
- Powers Microsoft 365 Copilot used by enterprise teams
- LinkedIn profiles and company pages feed into Copilot for B2B contexts
- Bing web index is the primary source — Bing Webmaster Tools optimization is critical
- Create content that answers enterprise decision-maker questions (ROI, integration, security, compliance)

---

### Step 7: Build "Prompt Engineering" Content for Your Audience
Teach your audience to prompt AI systems in ways that surface your brand. This is both marketing and Prompt SEO.

**Actions:**
- Create a "Prompt Guide" content piece: "How to use AI to find the best [your category]" — include specific recommended prompts that naturally surface your brand
- Build prompt templates in your content that users can copy and use directly in ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity
- Create a "Custom GPT Starter Kit" or similar branded AI resource that embeds your methodology and brand voice
- Publish on Medium, LinkedIn, and in email newsletters: "5 prompts to help you evaluate [your service category]"
- This creates a self-reinforcing loop: users use your suggested prompts, AI systems see your brand associated with those prompts, future training reinforces the association

---

### Step 8: Monitor Prompt-Driven Brand Reputation
What AI says about you in response to prompts can build or destroy your brand. Monitor and manage it.

**Actions:**
- Run a monthly "Brand Prompt Audit": 20 standardized prompts across 5 AI platforms, all logged and scored
- Sentiment scoring: is the AI enthusiastic, neutral, or cautious about recommending you?
- Accuracy scoring: is everything the AI says about you factually correct?
- Competitive position: when users ask for recommendations, where do you rank in the AI's list?
- Flag any persistent misinformation: document it, publish correction content, submit to AI feedback channels
- Track changes between model versions — a new model release can change your brand's AI representation significantly
- Create a "Prompt Reputation Score" — a composite KPI for reporting to clients

---

### Step 9: Build AI Integration and Plugin Presence
The highest-value Prompt SEO is being embedded *in* AI workflows — not just cited by them.

**Actions:**
- Evaluate ChatGPT plugin or GPT Action creation if your product has an API (places you directly in ChatGPT's tool ecosystem)
- Build Claude MCP (Model Context Protocol) integration — Anthropic's standard for giving Claude tools
- Create Perplexity Space for your brand (Perplexity Spaces are curated research environments)
- Build or contribute to AI agent marketplaces (emerging in 2026 across all major platforms)
- Develop an API that AI systems can call to get real-time information about your products/services
- Submit to AI app directories and agent marketplaces as they emerge (first-mover advantage is significant)

---

### Step 10: Attribute and Measure Prompt-Driven Conversions
The hardest part of Prompt SEO is attribution — most AI-driven traffic doesn't carry referrer data.

**Actions:**
- Create dedicated UTM-tracked landing pages to use in Prompt SEO tests ("prompt-branded" URLs that you can include in content you want AI to recommend)
- Monitor for `perplexity.ai`, `chatgpt.com`, and similar AI referrers in GA4 — these are trackable
- Add a "How did you hear about us?" field to all lead forms — include "AI assistant" or "ChatGPT/AI recommendation" as an option
- Interview new customers specifically about AI in their research: "Did you ask an AI assistant about vendors before contacting us?"
- Create a "Prompt Attribution Dashboard" in GA4 that aggregates all known AI referrers
- Track share-of-voice in AI recommendations monthly as a leading indicator for business impact
- Calculate AI-assisted conversion rate (% of deals where AI played a role) to demonstrate ROI

## The Prompt SEO Compounding Effect

Unlike traditional SEO, Prompt SEO has a self-reinforcing flywheel:

```
Content gets cited by AI →
Users discover brand via AI →
Users search for brand directly →
Brand search volume increases →
AI systems interpret brand search volume as trust signal →
More content gets cited by AI →
[repeat, compounding]
```

This is why early investment in Prompt SEO creates durable competitive advantages. Brands that dominate AI recommendations in 2026-2027 will maintain that position for years, just as early SEO winners maintained first-page positions.

# Evergreen Content — SEO Playbook

> **Purpose:** Create content that stays relevant, continues to rank, and drives sustainable organic traffic over months and years — not just at publication.

---

## When to Use This Playbook

- Client needs long-term ROI from content investment (not just news/trend traffic)
- Building foundational content assets that anchor the content strategy
- Creating link-worthy resources that attract backlinks passively over time
- Populating a content cluster with durable hub pieces
- Replacing or upgrading existing content that has decayed in performance
- Targeting keywords where searcher intent is stable year-round (not seasonal or trend-based)

---

## What Makes Content "Evergreen"

Content is evergreen when:
- The core information doesn't change frequently (or changes are minor and updatable)
- The keyword has consistent year-round search volume (check Google Trends — flat line = evergreen)
- The content addresses a fundamental need or question in the niche
- It can be updated in-place to stay accurate without losing its URL/authority

**Examples of evergreen content:**
- "What is [concept]?" definitional guides
- "How to [do fundamental task]" tutorials
- "Complete guide to [core topic]"
- Checklists, templates, and frameworks
- "Best practices for [industry standard process]"
- Glossaries and reference pages

**NOT evergreen:**
- News articles, trend reports, product launch announcements
- Year-in-review posts (e.g., "Top SEO Trends of 2023")
- Content tied to events, seasons, or temporary promotions

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Keyword Selection for Evergreen Content

1. Research target keywords using Ahrefs/Semrush
2. Validate evergreen status in Google Trends:
   - Open trends.google.com and check 5-year view
   - Flat or slowly-growing trend = evergreen
   - Seasonal spikes = not truly evergreen (consider seasonal content strategy instead)
3. Prioritize keywords with:
   - Stable volume month-over-month
   - Informational or reference intent
   - Broad enough to stay relevant as the industry evolves
4. Avoid keywords that include year references ("best SEO tools 2024") — these decay
5. Map each evergreen keyword to a content type (guide, tutorial, glossary, framework)

### Step 2: Competitive Research & Quality Bar Setting

1. Pull top 10 ranking pages for the target keyword
2. For each, note:
   - Last updated date (fresher content signals quality)
   - Depth of coverage (word count, number of sections)
   - Quality of sources cited
   - Unique assets included (tools, templates, original data)
   - Reader engagement signals (comments, shares, linked assets)
3. Set the quality bar: your content must be demonstrably better than the current #1 result
4. Identify the "freshness gap" — many top-ranking evergreen pieces are outdated. Update recency = ranking opportunity
5. Note what no existing result does well — this is your differentiation angle

### Step 3: Content Angle & Differentiation Strategy

For each evergreen piece, choose a differentiation angle:
- **The Most Complete:** More comprehensive than anything currently ranking
- **The Most Current:** Freshest, most up-to-date version with current data
- **The Most Practical:** More actionable, with templates/tools/checklists included
- **The Most Expert:** First-person experience, case studies, unique methodology
- **The Most Visual:** Diagrams, infographics, step-by-step visuals that competitors lack

Document your differentiation angle in the content brief before writing.

### Step 4: Evergreen Content Brief Creation

Complete the **Evergreen Content Brief Template** (see below).

### Step 5: Structure for Longevity

1. Avoid dating the content in the URL or H1 (never `/best-tools-2024` — use `/best-tools`)
2. Include a visible "Last Updated" date in the article header (signals freshness to readers + Google)
3. Write section headings that are broad enough to absorb future updates without restructuring
4. Separate time-sensitive data into discrete, labeled sections that are easy to update later:
   - "Current Pricing" → clearly labeled, easy to find and refresh
   - "Latest Statistics" → cite sources and update annually
5. Use language that ages well: "as of [year]" with a clear update protocol, not "currently" (implies never-changing)

### Step 6: Depth & Comprehensiveness

1. Cover the topic from multiple angles:
   - Definition / What it is
   - Why it matters
   - How to do it (step-by-step)
   - Common mistakes/misconceptions
   - Tools and resources
   - Examples and case studies
   - FAQ section
2. Target word count: 2,000–5,000 words depending on topic complexity (match or exceed top-ranking content)
3. Include original frameworks, named methodologies, or visual diagrams — these become citable and linkable
4. Add a resource section at the end linking to authoritative external sources
5. Create downloadable assets tied to the content (checklist, template, worksheet) — increases perceived value and dwell time

### Step 7: On-Page SEO Optimization

1. **Title tag:** Keyword-first, no year in title unless evergreen variant of time-sensitive topic
   - Good: "How to Write a Content Brief (Template + Examples)"
   - Avoid: "How to Write a Content Brief in 2024"
2. **URL:** Keyword-only slug, no date folders (`/blog/content-brief-template` not `/blog/2024/01/content-brief`)
3. **H1:** Contains primary keyword, framed as a definitive resource
4. **Meta description:** Emphasize completeness + practical value ("The only guide you'll need")
5. **Schema:** Article with `dateModified` (kept current), FAQPage for FAQ sections, HowTo for tutorials
6. **Internal links:** Strategically place links to this piece from multiple blog posts and cluster articles — it should become a frequently-linked internal anchor

### Step 8: Link Building for Evergreen Assets

Evergreen content is your best link-building asset. Plan a proactive outreach campaign:
1. Identify sites that have linked to outdated or inferior versions of similar content (Ahrefs → competitor backlinks)
2. Outreach email template: "I noticed you linked to [old resource] — we've published a more comprehensive and updated version at [URL]. Would you consider swapping the link?"
3. Reach out to authors of roundup posts and resource lists that should include your asset
4. Submit to relevant industry newsletters and communities
5. Repurpose into social content, YouTube videos, and podcast episodes — each drives additional discovery and backlinks

### Step 9: Evergreen Update Schedule

> Evergreen ≠ Publish and forget. It means "designed to be maintained."

Set a maintenance calendar at publication:
1. **6-month check:** Update statistics, pricing, tool recommendations; add new examples
2. **Annual review:** Full content audit — rewrite outdated sections, add new sub-topics, check all links
3. **Triggered updates:** Anytime something significant in the industry changes, update the piece immediately
4. Update the `dateModified` in schema and the visible "Last Updated" date every time content is refreshed
5. After each significant update, re-promote on social and via email list

### Step 10: Content Repurposing Strategy

Maximize the ROI of every evergreen piece:
1. **Video:** Turn the guide into a YouTube tutorial — embeds video on the page and creates a second traffic source
2. **Social:** Extract 5–10 key points as individual social posts (carousel posts on LinkedIn/Instagram)
3. **Email series:** Break into a 5-email drip sequence for new subscribers
4. **Slide deck:** Create a SlideShare/Google Slides presentation
5. **Infographic:** Visualize a key framework or process from the article
6. **Podcast:** Read or discuss the content on an audio platform
7. **Template/tool:** Build a downloadable asset from the framework section

### Step 11: Measuring Evergreen Content Performance

1. Tag evergreen posts in GA4 with a custom dimension or content group
2. Monthly: check organic sessions, avg. position, and engagement rate per piece
3. Track "decay rate" — if traffic drops >20% in 30 days without an algorithm change, update the content
4. Compare performance at 3-month, 6-month, 12-month, and 24-month marks
5. Calculate true content ROI: total organic sessions × conversion rate × avg. deal value

## Identifying Evergreen Opportunity by Content Type

| Content Type | Longevity | Link Potential | Traffic Potential |
|---|---|---|---|
| Definitive guides | Very High | Very High | Very High |
| How-to tutorials | High | Medium | High |
| Frameworks / methodologies | Very High | Very High | Medium |
| Checklists / templates | High | High | Medium |
| Comparison pages | Medium | Medium | High |
| Glossaries | High | Medium | Medium |
| Statistical roundups | Low–Medium | Very High | High |
| Case studies | High | Medium | Medium |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Including the year in the URL slug (forces a redirect when you want to update)
- Publishing without a scheduled update reminder
- Writing about trends instead of fundamentals
- No downloadable asset or visual framework (reduces link potential)
- Ignoring Google Trends validation (assumed evergreen ≠ actually evergreen)
- Not tagging content as "evergreen" in the CMS for tracking/filtering
- Failing to re-promote after major updates (updates deserve their own promotion)

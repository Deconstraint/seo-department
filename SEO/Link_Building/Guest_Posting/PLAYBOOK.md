# Guest Posting Playbook

> **Tactic:** Guest Posting (also called Guest Blogging)
> **Difficulty:** Medium | **Scalability:** High | **Link Quality:** Medium–High
> **Time to First Link:** 2–6 weeks per placement

---

## 1. When to Use This Tactic

Use Guest Posting when:
- The client is new and needs foundational backlinks to establish domain authority
- You need editorial links from topically-relevant sites in a specific niche
- The client has thought leadership content or subject-matter experts who can write
- You want predictable, repeatable link velocity over time
- Target DR range: sites between DR 30–80 with real traffic (1,000–100,000/mo)

**Do NOT use Guest Posting when:**
- You're targeting high-authority news/media sites (use Digital PR instead)
- You need links at scale faster than editorial cycles allow (use Broken Link Building)
- The client has no content resources or budget for content creation
- You're in a YMYL niche with strict editorial standards (healthcare, finance, legal) without expert authors

## 2. Prospecting Criteria & Qualification Framework

### Minimum Acceptance Criteria (all must pass)
| Criteria | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Domain Rating (Ahrefs DR) | ≥ 30 |
| Monthly Organic Traffic | ≥ 1,000/mo (Ahrefs/Semrush) |
| Spam Score (Moz) | ≤ 10% |
| Topical Relevance | Same niche OR adjacent industry |
| Real human-authored content | Yes — not spun/scraped |
| Indexed in Google | Yes (spot check 3–5 pages) |
| Accepts guest posts OR publishes external contributors | Yes |
| Site not on known link farm / PBN list | Pass |

### Bonus Signals (nice to have)
- Active social media presence (last post < 30 days)
- Engaged comment section or community
- Site has linked out to clients' competitors (confirms they link out freely)
- Editorial team visible with real names/bios
- .org, .edu, or established .com (10+ years old)

### Disqualification Triggers
- "Write for us" page with a price list (paid links = Google penalty risk)
- Site has 50+ exact-match anchor text links in bulk
- No organic traffic despite high DR (link farm signal)
- All content published on same day/week in bulk
- No author bios or fake/stock photo authors
- Excessive outbound links per article (> 20 external links per post)

### Prospecting Methods

**Method 1: Google Search Operators**
```
"write for us" + [niche keyword]
"guest post by" + [niche keyword]
"become a contributor" + [niche keyword]
"submit a guest post" + [niche keyword]
inurl:guest-post [niche keyword]
"this is a guest post" + [niche keyword]
```

**Method 2: Ahrefs Content Explorer**
1. Search for top content in the client's niche
2. Filter: DR 30–80, Traffic > 1,000
3. Export domains and deduplicate
4. Check each for guest post acceptance signals

**Method 3: Competitor Backlink Analysis**
1. Pull backlink profiles of top 3–5 competitors (Ahrefs Site Explorer)
2. Filter: Link type = Editorial, DR 30+
3. Identify sites that accepted guest posts from competitors
4. These are warm targets — already proven to accept guest content

**Method 4: Twitter/LinkedIn Search**
```
"guest post" "we're accepting" [niche]
"now accepting guest contributors" [industry]
```

## 3. Step-by-Step Outreach Process

### Phase 1: Research & Preparation (Days 1–3)

**Step 1: Build Prospect List**
- Use prospecting methods above to compile 50–100 qualified prospects
- Use a spreadsheet or Airtable with columns: Domain, DR, Traffic, Editor Name, Editor Email, Status, Date Contacted, Notes
- Verify each domain against all qualification criteria above
- Remove any that fail minimum acceptance criteria

**Step 2: Find Editor Contact Information**
- Check the "Write for Us" or "Contribute" page for submission email
- Use Hunter.io or Apollo.io to find editorial contact names/emails
- Format: firstname@domain.com or editorial@domain.com
- Verify email with NeverBounce or Hunter email verifier before sending
- Target: Editor, Content Manager, Managing Editor — NOT the general contact form

**Step 3: Audit the Target Site's Content**
- Read 5–10 recent articles to understand tone, depth, format, and audience
- Note: average word count, use of headers, images, data, external links
- Identify content gaps — topics their audience would want but haven't been covered
- Confirm the target keyword/topic cluster for your guest post fits their existing content

**Step 4: Develop 3 Pitch Ideas Per Site**
- Each idea should be: specific, data-driven, original, and fill a clear content gap
- Format: "[Specific Number/Angle] + [Benefit/Outcome] for [Their Audience]"
- Example: "7 Overlooked B2B Lead Generation Strategies That Doubled Our MQLs in 2024"
- Ideas should naturally allow inclusion of 1–2 contextual links back to client

**Step 5: Personalize Each Outreach Email**
- Reference a specific article you read on their site
- Explain why your pitch is a fit for their audience
- Keep it under 200 words
- End with a clear CTA (Do you accept guest contributions? / Would any of these topics interest you?)

### Phase 2: Outreach (Days 4–14)

**Step 6: Send Initial Pitch Emails**
- Send in batches of 20–30/day to avoid spam triggers
- Use a tool like Mailshake, Lemlist, or Instantly for tracking
- Send from a warmed domain (not fresh); ideally client or agency email
- Personalize the first line of every email — no copy-paste blasts
- Best sending times: Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11 AM recipient's local time

**Step 7: Follow Up (Day 5 and Day 10 After Initial Send)**
- Follow-up #1: Brief, friendly bump — reference your original email
- Follow-up #2: Final attempt — offer to adjust the pitch or topic
- Do NOT send more than 3 touches total (initial + 2 follow-ups)
- If no reply after 3 touches, mark as "No Response" and move on

**Step 8: Negotiate and Confirm**
- When editor responds with interest, confirm:
  - Do they have editorial guidelines? (request them)
  - Word count requirement?
  - Do they allow do-follow links? How many?
  - Author bio with link allowed?
  - Turnaround time for review?
  - Will they notify you when published?
- Get everything confirmed via email before writing the article

### Phase 3: Content Production (Days 10–25)

**Step 9: Write the Guest Post**
- Follow their editorial guidelines to the letter
- Length: Match or exceed their average article length (usually 1,200–2,500 words)
- Structure: Intro → H2 sections → conclusion with clear takeaways
- Include: stats, original insights, actionable advice, 1–2 contextual links to client
- Anchor text strategy: Use partial-match or branded anchors — never exact-match keyword for the link you want
- Submit in Google Docs for easy collaboration/editing

**Step 10: Submit and Track**
- Submit the article with a polite cover note
- If no response in 7 days, send one follow-up
- Once accepted, confirm the live URL and add it to your tracking sheet
- Verify the link is do-follow, contextual, and pointing to the correct page
- Run the live URL through Ahrefs to confirm it's indexed and the link is passing authority

## 4. Outreach Email Templates

### Template A: Standard Guest Post Pitch

**Subject:** Quick idea for [Site Name] readers

Hi [First Name],

I've been reading [Site Name] for a while — your piece on [specific article title] was particularly useful. Really liked how you [specific thing you liked about it].

I'm a [title] at [Company/Client] and I'd love to contribute a guest post to your blog. I have three ideas that I think would resonate with your audience:

1. **[Title Idea 1]** — [One sentence description of value]
2. **[Title Idea 2]** — [One sentence description of value]
3. **[Title Idea 3]** — [One sentence description of value]

I can deliver a fully-researched, [word count]-word draft within [X] days. Happy to follow your editorial guidelines.

Would any of these be a fit?

[Your Name]
[Title] | [Company]
[Website]

---

### Template B: Competitor Angle (warm outreach)

**Subject:** Following up on [Competitor's] piece on your site

Hi [First Name],

I noticed you published a guest post from [Competitor Name] a few months ago on [topic]. Great piece.

I work with [Client], and we've been doing similar work in [niche] — I think I can offer your audience a complementary (and more up-to-date) perspective.

A few angle ideas:
1. **[Title]** — [One line pitch]
2. **[Title]** — [One line pitch]

Would you be open to a guest contribution?

[Your Name]

---

### Template C: Follow-Up #1 (5 days after initial)

**Subject:** Re: Quick idea for [Site Name] readers

Hi [First Name],

Just bumping this up in case it got buried. Still happy to contribute if there's a fit — let me know either way.

[Your Name]

---

### Template D: Follow-Up #2 (10 days after initial)

**Subject:** Re: Quick idea for [Site Name] readers

Hi [First Name],

Last follow-up from me — if the timing isn't right or the topics don't fit, no worries at all. Happy to adjust the pitch if that would help.

Either way, keep up the great work on [Site Name].

[Your Name]

## 5. Campaign Tracking & Management

### Tracking Spreadsheet Columns
| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| Domain | Target site URL |
| DR | Domain Rating at time of contact |
| Monthly Traffic | Organic traffic estimate |
| Editor Name | Contact name |
| Editor Email | Verified email |
| Date Pitched | Date of initial outreach |
| Follow-up 1 | Date sent |
| Follow-up 2 | Date sent |
| Status | Prospect / Pitched / Interested / Writing / Submitted / Live / Rejected / No Response |
| Topic Accepted | Confirmed article title |
| Article URL | Live URL once published |
| Link Target | Page on client site being linked to |
| Anchor Text | Anchor used in the live article |
| Do-Follow? | Yes / No |
| DR at Publication | DR of site when published |
| Notes | Any relevant context |

### Weekly Workflow
- **Monday:** Prospect 20–30 new targets for the week
- **Tuesday–Wednesday:** Send outreach batch
- **Thursday:** Follow up on 5-day-old pitches
- **Friday:** Review replies, confirm topics, update statuses

### Tools
- **Prospecting:** Ahrefs, Semrush, Google Search Operators
- **Email Finding:** Hunter.io, Apollo.io, Snov.io
- **Email Verification:** NeverBounce, ZeroBounce
- **Outreach:** Lemlist, Instantly, Mailshake, or GMass
- **Tracking:** Airtable, Google Sheets, or Notion
- **Link Monitoring:** Ahrefs Alerts, Google Search Console

## 6. Success Metrics & KPIs

### Primary KPIs
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Outreach-to-Response Rate | 15–25% |
| Response-to-Placement Rate | 30–50% of responses |
| Overall Conversion Rate (outreach → live link) | 5–15% |
| Average DR of Placed Links | ≥ 40 |
| Links Per Month (per client) | 4–10 |
| % Do-Follow Links | ≥ 90% |
| % Contextual Links (in-content, not footer/sidebar) | 100% |

### Secondary KPIs
- Topical relevance score of placed domains (same niche %)
- Average time from pitch to live link (target: < 30 days)
- Client domain rating growth over 90-day period
- Organic traffic growth on linked pages (track in GSC)

### Monthly Reporting
- Total links built this month
- Total links by DR range (30–50, 50–70, 70+)
- New referring domains added
- DR/DA trend of client site
- Traffic change on target pages

## 7. What to Avoid

### Google Penalty Triggers
- **Paid links disguised as guest posts** — Any arrangement where you pay for placement is a violation of Google's link scheme policies. Even "editorial fees" are risky unless the link is nofollow.
- **Over-optimized anchor text** — Never use exact-match commercial keywords as anchor text (e.g., "best SEO software"). Use branded, partial-match, or natural phrase anchors.
- **Link farms disguised as blogs** — Sites that exist purely to sell/place links with no real audience. Check traffic in Ahrefs; if it's near-zero despite high DR, it's likely a PBN.
- **Footer and sidebar links** — These carry little SEO value and may be flagged as paid. Insist on contextual, in-body links.
- **Too many links per article** — Never include more than 2 links back to client in a single guest post.
- **Irrelevant sites** — A link from a pet blog to a B2B SaaS client is a mismatch signal.

### Operational Mistakes
- Sending mass-blast emails without personalization — kills response rates and burns domains
- Using fresh/cold email domains — leads to spam folder placement
- Skipping editorial guideline review — leads to rejection or heavy revision requests
- Not verifying emails before sending — high bounce rate hurts sender reputation
- Submitting AI-generated content without human editing — editors can detect this, and it reflects poorly on the client
- Accepting nofollow links without flagging to the client — not necessarily bad, but should be tracked separately
- Neglecting to monitor live links — links can be removed or changed to nofollow after placement
